Q. What can we say about the coupled system from the single neudel phase portrait?
A. In my opinion, the balance between the number of inhibitory/excitatory neudel generates spikes (as the very last plot from each simulation)
Q. Are there levels of input (or interaction) for which such description is relevant to understand the global dynamics?
A. We need populations of neurons rather than a neudel to understand the global dynamics.
Q. How to characterize the behavior of the global system?
A. The spikes are closely related to the interactions between excitatory and inhibitory neurons.
Q. What if we increase the number of neudels in the network?
A. I think a global pattern (some slow ratios between spikes) emerges.
  1. num of Inhibitory neudel (0) < excitatory neudel (2)

III. AdEx_Network.py

Q. What can we call the network behavior? What can we measure the network behavior? How?
A. By calculating the correlation between different groups of neurons.
Q. Can we see correlation between them?
A. The correlation coefficient between the population rate between the Fast spiking neuron group and the regular spiking neuron group is 0.97. Therefore, it is highly correlated.
Q. What type of information can we obtain on the dynamics?
A. Different types of neurons, firing rate, number of neurons, strengths of their connection influence the dynamics.
Q. What kind of relationships can be made with experimental data? What are the limitations?
A. It may hard to observe a robust influence of stimuli in an experiment because the behavior of neurons are the outcome of various factor of neurons (neronal types, the strength of connection, firing rate, connectivities between different groups of neurons, etc).
